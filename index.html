London

I currently study in London, and it is safe to say it is one of the most vibrant and entertaining cities in the world. People from a variety of backgrounds and nationalities unite here to work, study and live a good life. One can never be bored in London, as there's something for everyone, whether it be live musicals, a range of shopping choices, a variety of restaurants and numerous tourist spots. If you have some time, head down to the Harry Potter Studios for a magical experience as well!

Places to visit:

1. Covent Garden

2. London Eye

3. Chinatown

4. Oxford Street

Food to try:

1. Fish and chips

2. English breakfast

3. Yorkshire pudding

Goa

Relaxing beaches, fun parties, and a laidback environment are what one thinks of when they think of Goa, and it is safe to say that Goa serves all this and more. The city provides a great relief from the stress real life provides,and is a safe haven if you wish to truly enjoy a peaceful life. Goa is a way of living, and an experience everyone must have, and don't forget to experience delicious Goan delicacies along the way!

On the way to the city center. Transport around Hong Kong is easily accessible. The city is connected with a good metro and railway system, and also has well-built roads. One can also enjoy a scenic boat ride and watch the views of Hong Kong's iconic landscape. However, watch out for traffic in the city's busiest spots!

Hong Kong Disneyland. This the place to be for adults and children alike if you love adventure, Disney fan or not! Due to its compact size, it is easy to cover the park in a day's time. Apart from the rides, my personal favourite was the mini Lion King musical performance, perfect for a rainy day when most rides are suspended!

Monastery at Lantau Island. Apart from the shopping, amusement parks and general tourist destinations, head to Lantau Island for a truly spiritually energizing experience. The monastery is extremely peaceful, and there is no place like Lantau if you're seeking a few blessings.

The famous Tower Bridge is perfect if you fancy a stroll along the River Thames on a breezy evening. The scenic views, the beautiful London weather and the grand architecture offer you a firsthand London experience. You can also visit the Tower of London, which is officially Her Majesty's Royal Palace, and visit the souvenir shop after to pick up some royal memorablia to take home!

If you have some extra time, the Harry Potter Studios on the outskirts of London are definitely a must visit. The studios take you through a tour of all the important props, sets and scenes from the famous Harry Potter movies. The studios also privde a general idea about film direction, and is thus an attractive destination for all movie lovers.

The majestic St. Paul's Cathedral is 1,400 years old, and is a wonderful example of an Anglican cathedral. It is the seat of the Bishop of London, and its dome is one of the highest in the world. For approximately 15 pounds, one can enter the Cathedral to view its astounding beauty and intricate designs. The Cathedral provides a perfect site to click beautiful pictures.

I would highly suggest driving around in order to get a comprehensive view of the true Goan life. Hiring a driver or renting a car are not too expensive here, and it allows you to drive through villages, under lush trees and past breezy beaches. Since the Goa city centre is relatively small, this is a perfect idea for a relaxed holiday.

I stayed at the Cidade de Goa resort in Goa which comes with its own private beach, and would highly recommend any tourist in need of some relaxation and time away from the hustle and bustle to do so. This humble resort provides fantastic views of the beach and a perfect opportunity to stroll along the waters without disturbance.

Goa is incomplete without its delicious food! Make sure to try all the local delicacies such as xacuti and vindaloo. The picture depicts a quiet evening enjoying some paneer pakodas and drinks, proving that no matter what you eat, it is always a good time in Goa!
